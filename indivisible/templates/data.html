About the Data

Sites we used

Our data comes from a number of different sources. Many of these sources offered free APIs (which make data extraction and re-use easy), and others, while free, don't offer a good method of aggregation. We tried to take the pain out of keeping track of all these data sources, but for those who are interested, here they are.

ProPublica OpenCongress API ProPublica offers a number of free data sources as part of their mission. The Congress API serves as the base of this site, providing biographical info, voting records, bill sponsorships, public statements, and committee membership.
GovTrack The site I would use if this one didn't exist. GovTrack presents bills and vote information in an easily navigable and searchable format. It is effectively a wrapper around Congress.gov.
On the Issues Provides analyses of each politician on major issues, using their past statements and voting records as a guide.
The Government Publishing Office The GPO produces a member guide that includes office and staff information for members of Congress. They haven't added the 115th Congress yet, but I will update the site when they do.
Senate.gov Contains the Senate calendar (including committee schedules), as well as information on active legislation (mostly links to Congress.gov) and some historical info.
House.gov Contains the House calendar and information on upcoming legislation.
Event Registry A free news aggregation site that groups news articles by entities (e.g. people, events, etc), forming a stream of 'news events' for each entity
Politifact A website that does fact checking of statements made by and about politicians.

Sites we recommend

These are sites whose data, for one reason or another, I wasn't able to use. Typically this had to do with access costs, but sometimes it had to do with not being able to easily generate links to the relevant pages.

Vote Smart Similarly to On the Issues, Vote Smart attempts to infer policy positions for politicians based on votes and statements. I particularly like their "ratings" tabs, which lists endorsements from organizations. Those endorsements typically contain further fact sheets which break down the reason for the rating or endorsement, which can be enlightening.
Open Secrets Provides donor information for politicians.
